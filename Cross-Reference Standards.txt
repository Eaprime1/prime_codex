## SECTION 7: QUALITY ASSURANCE & CALIBRATION

### Common Assessment Challenges

**Challenge 1: "I can't be objective about my own work"**  
Solution: Use multiple assessors, compare scores, discuss discrepancies.

**Challenge 2: "Scores vary wildly between assessors"**  
Solution: Review rubric together, establish shared understanding of criteria.

**Challenge 3: "This feels too rigid/quantitative"**  
Solution: Use scores as starting points for qualitative discussion, not absolute truth.

**Challenge 4: "Components seem interdependent"**  
Solution: They are! But assess each individually first, then synthesize.

**Challenge 5: "My entity doesn't fit these categories well"**  
Solution: Adapt rubrics to context, but maintain core assessment questions.

---

### Calibration Protocol

To ensure consistency across assessors:

**Step 1: Benchmark Assessment**  
Everyone assesses the same well-known entity independently.

**Step 2: Score Comparison**  
Share scores, identify discrepancies (>0.2 difference).

**Step 3: Discussion**  
Review rubric criteria, discuss interpretation differences.

**Step 4: Re-Assessment**  
Assess same entity again after discussion.

**Step 5: Validation**  
Scores should now be within 0.1 of each other.

**Recommended Benchmark Entities**:
- Seventh Pinnacle mathematics (should score 0.85-0.95)
- Triadic thinking framework (should score 0.75-0.85)
- A recent experimental conversation (should score 0.40-0.60)

---

When multiple people assess the same entity:
