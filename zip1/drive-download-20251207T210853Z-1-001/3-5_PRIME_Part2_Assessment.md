## IV. AMPLIFICATION FACTOR (AF) MEASUREMENT

### The Critical Metric

**Amplification Factor** = Output Value / Sum of Input Values

This is THE quantitative measure of synergy.

### Measurement Methodology

#### Step 1: Define Output Value

**Quantitative Measures** (when applicable):
- Revenue generated
- Users served
- Problems solved
- Papers published
- Patents filed
- Lines of code
- Documents created

**Qualitative Measures** (when quantitative not applicable):
- Expert assessment of quality
- Peer review ratings
- User satisfaction scores
- Innovation rating (1-10 scale)
- Impact assessment

**Time-Based Proxy**:
- "How many person-hours would this take individually?"
- Compare to actual collaborative hours
- Ratio = Amplification Factor

#### Step 2: Measure Input Values

**Individual Contributions**:
- Time invested by each participant
- Resources contributed
- Expertise applied
- Prior work brought

**Sum all inputs** in comparable units

#### Step 3: Calculate AF

**AF = Output / Total Input**

#### Step 4: Interpret

**AF â‰¤ 1.0**: Collaboration is overhead (negative synergy)  
**AF = 1.0**: Purely additive (3-prime)  
**AF = 1.1-1.5**: Early synergy (transition)  
**AF = 1.5-2.5**: Solid synergy (5-prime)  
**AF = 2.5-4.0**: Strong synergy (mature 5-prime)  
**AF > 4.0**: Exceptional synergy (approaching limits)

### Example Calculations

#### Example 1: Research Paper

**Inputs**:
- Researcher A: 100 hours
- Researcher B: 100 hours
- Researcher C: 100 hours
- Total: 300 hours

**Output**:
- Paper equivalent to 450 hours of individual work (expert assessment)

**AF** = 450 / 300 = **1.5**

**Interpretation**: Solid 5-prime operation. The collaboration generated 50% more value than sum of inputs.

#### Example 2: Software Development

**Inputs**:
- Developer 1: 40 hours
- Developer 2: 40 hours
- Total: 80 hours

**Output**:
- Code equivalent to 250 hours individual work (lines, complexity, quality assessment)

**AF** = 250 / 80 = **3.125**

**Interpretation**: Strong 5-prime. Collaboration more than tripled efficiency through synergistic design thinking.

#### Example 3: Creative Project

**Inputs**:
- Artist: 60 hours
- Writer: 60 hours
- Musician: 60 hours
- Total: 180 hours

**Output**:
- Multimedia work rated 9.5/10 innovation
- Individual work averages 6/10
- Quality ratio: 9.5/6 = 1.58
- Applied to time: 180 Ã— 1.58 = 285 equivalent hours

**AF** = 285 / 180 = **1.58**

**Interpretation**: Solid 5-prime with quality amplification through cross-disciplinary synergy.

### Phenomenological Interview Questions

**Individual Interview** (30-45 minutes):
